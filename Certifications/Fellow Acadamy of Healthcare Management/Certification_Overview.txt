Fellow, Academy for Healthcare Management (FAHM) – Completed 03/06/2024


Earned the FAHM designation from the Academy for Healthcare Management (AHIP), recognizes expertise in health plan operations and managed care. The program requires successful completion of coursework and exams across five core domains:

•	Healthcare management fundamentals (AHM 250) – Structure and evolution of health plans, plan types (HMOs, PPOs, ACOs), provider organizations, utilization management, quality programs, and regulatory landscape.


•	Governance, legal issues, Medicare & Medicaid (AHM 510) – Health plan governance models, state and federal regulation, ERISA, ACA, compliance, fraud/waste/abuse, and the role of Medicare and Medicaid in health plan design and oversight. 


•	Health plan finance and risk (AHM 520) – Financial statements, cost and managerial accounting, performance measures, pricing and underwriting, capital and risk management, reserving, and funding strategies for health plans.  


•	Network management (AHM 530) – Network design and adequacy, provider contracting and reimbursement, provider performance, pharmacy networks, and strategies to build and maintain high-performing provider networks.


•	Medical management (AHM 540) – Utilization review, case and disease management, quality measurement and improvement, benefit/coverage policy, pharmacy benefit management, and alignment of clinical programs with cost and quality goals. 


This designation positions with cross-functional understanding of how governance, regulation, clinical programs, provider networks, and financial risk all interact within health plans. It demonstrates the ability to:
•	Interpret and navigate regulatory and compliance requirements for health plans
•	Understand and analyze health plan financial performance and risk
•	Engage with medical management and utilization strategies that balance quality, cost, and member experience
•	Work effectively with provider networks, pharmacy partners, and operational teams to support sustainable, value-based care models

There is currently no maintenance period defined for this designation.
